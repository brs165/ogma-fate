At the Table

Running your session with Ogma

Ogma is designed to be used during live play — not just in prep. This guide covers the tools that make it a table surface, not just a generator.

Projector Mode — your second screen

Ogma can push results to a second screen — a TV, projector, or secondary monitor — so your players see the same information without looking at your GM screen.

Setting up Projector Mode

  1. Open projector.html on your display screenNavigate to projector.html in a separate browser tab or window, then move it to your second screen and go fullscreen (F11). The screen will show "Waiting for GM to push a result…"
  2. Roll a result in OgmaUse any campaign page as normal. Roll any generator.
  3. Press V or click 📺The result appears on the projector instantly. Use the Sidebar → At the Table → 📺 Projector link to open the projector screen if you haven't yet.
🔌 How the sync works

On the same device, Ogma uses the browser's BroadcastChannel API for instant same-tab sync. A localStorage polling fallback (500ms) handles cross-tab and file:// scenarios. Both screens must be open in the same browser on the same device.

Live interactive sync

The projector isn't just a static display. When you interact with certain result cards, the projector updates in real time:

  • Countdown boxes — tap to fill on your screen, fills animate on the projector. When the last box is marked, the clock triggers with a shake animation and the outcome appears.
  • Contest scores — tap to mark victories. The projector shows both tracks filling in blue (Side A) and red (Side B). When someone wins, a banner appears.
  • Scene aspects — tap an aspect in a Scene result to spotlight it. It jumps to the top on the projector, highlighted, while non-active aspects dim. Players see exactly what's in play.
  • NPC stress tracks — mark stress on a Major or Minor NPC and the projector's track fills. When all boxes are marked — TAKEN OUT overlay fires.

Fate Point pool strip

The bottom of the projector always shows the party's current fate point pool as glowing dots. This matches the SRD recommendation that the fate economy should be visible to all players. Dots animate when points are spent or earned.

Fate Point Tracker

Click the ◎ button in the action bar to open the FP Tracker. It has three tabs:

  • Fate Points — tracks the party pool (shared points), per-PC points, and per-NPC points. Each PC has a Refresh value; clicking ↺ resets everyone to refresh.
  • Milestones — Minor Milestone (end of every session) and Major Milestone (end of a story arc) checklists. Check items off as the group achieves them; tick marks animate.
  • Popcorn Initiative — the Balsera-style turn order. Add participants, then pass the token by clicking "Goes next →". No fixed turn order — the acting character picks who goes next.
🎪 Scenario: Tracking a tense contest

You've rolled a Contest — two gangs racing to reach the weapons cache. You push it to the projector with V. As each exchange resolves, you tap the victory boxes on your screen. Players watch the left track (Crimson Scars) and right track (Iron Saints) filling up on the TV. On the third tie, the twist aspect you rolled — "The Cache is Already Open" — gets revealed. No need to describe who's winning — everyone saw it happen.

Inspiration Mode

Press I to enter Inspiration Mode. Ogma rolls three results simultaneously. They display as overlapping ghost cards. Click the one you want to use — the others fade out. If none work, press I again for three more. Useful when a single roll isn't sparking anything creative.

Full Session prep packet

In the Session tab of the sidebar, click Full Session. Ogma generates three connected cards simultaneously:

  • Adventure Seed — location, objective, complication, 3-scene sketch
  • Countdown — a threat clock already ticking from session start
  • Compel — a ready-to-offer complication for the first scene

These three cards together are a Session 1 prep packet — enough to run 2–3 hours of play with no other prep. Click "Pin all" to save all three to History at once.

History and pinning

Every roll goes into a history ring (last 8 results). Pinned cards persist until you remove them. Open History with the 📋 button in the action bar or press the keyboard shortcut.

From History you can:

  • Re-view any card in full
  • Unpin individual cards
  • Copy all pinned cards as a single Markdown document
  • Export all NPCs as a Fari App batch import

Press Z to immediately undo the last pin without opening History.

Campaign Guide pages

Each campaign world has a dedicated Guide page (accessible from the sidebar under Navigate → Campaign Guide). These pages contain:

  • World tone and atmosphere
  • Player-facing setting summary
  • Key factions
  • Sample troubles, locations, and inspirations
  • Full content overview (table entry counts by category)

Guide pages are printable — use Ctrl+P to print a reference sheet for your players.
