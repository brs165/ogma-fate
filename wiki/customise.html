Customise

Making Ogma work for your table

Ogma adapts to different experience levels, play styles, and table setups. Here's how to tune it to your needs.

Help Levels

When you first open a campaign page, Ogma asks for your experience level. This sets the Help Level, which controls the depth of inline guidance in the GM Tips tab:

LevelWho it's forWhat changes
New to TTRPGs 🌿First-time GMsFull explanations, worked examples, extra context on why mechanics work
Coming from D&D ⚔Experienced D&D playersD&D comparisons unlocked, "D&D Notes" tab appears in result panel
Other RPGs 🎲RPG players new to FateFate-specific concepts explained without assuming D&D background
Knows Fate 🎲Experienced Fate playersMinimal help — just the generated content, no tutorials

Change your Help Level at any time from the sidebar (the HL chip in the topbar) or Settings.

GM Mode

The GM Mode toggle (in the sidebar, under At the Table) controls whether the GM Tips tab is visible in the result panel. Turn it off if players are watching your screen and you don't want the tips visible. The rest of the result card (aspects, skills, stress) is always shown.

Table Manager — customising results

Open the Table Manager with the ⊞ button in the action bar. It shows every table that feeds the current generator.

  • Exclude an entry by toggling the checkbox — it won't appear in future rolls. Useful when you've already used that NPC name or faction descriptor.
  • Lock an entry to always use it — roll any number of times and that entry will be the one that appears for that slot. Useful for fixed character names.
  • Add custom entries — type your own entry and add it to the table. It participates in generation alongside the built-in content.

Table customisations are saved to localStorage and persist across browser restarts.

Universal table merge

Ogma has a set of Universal tables — genre-neutral content that works in any campaign. The Universal Merge toggle (in Settings) controls whether these tables supplement the campaign-specific ones:

  • On (default) — universal entries are added to the campaign pool. More variety, slightly less world-specific flavour.
  • Off — only campaign-specific entries. Maximum thematic consistency.

Session Zero wizard

The Session Zero wizard (accessible from the sidebar under Navigate → Session Zero) guides a group through collaborative character creation. It works solo (for pre-gen prep) or as a group activity on a shared screen.

The wizard covers: the game pitch, safety tools, character concepts, the Phase Trio (aspects from backstory + crossing paths), skills, stunts, and refresh. At the end it generates a world issue and impending issue from the PC backstories.

D&D Transition guide

The D&D Transition guide explains every major mechanical difference between Fate Condensed and D&D — side by side, in plain language. Available from the topbar navigation on all pages.

Theme and text size

Press the ◑ button in the topbar to toggle between dark and light mode. Your preference is saved to localStorage.

In Settings (⚙ in the sidebar), you can also adjust text size between three levels. Useful for projector use or accessibility needs.

Keyboard shortcuts

Press ? to open the full keyboard shortcuts reference. All shortcuts are single-key (no modifier required) and are blocked when a text input is focused.
