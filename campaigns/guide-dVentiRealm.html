⚔ dVenti Realm

The Senate Collapsed. The Vaults Are Still Here.

The Arcane Senate fell thirty years ago. The dungeon licences expired. The constructs are still running thirty-year-old directives. The phylactery registry is sealed and nobody knows where the key went. Kobolds, dragons, liches, guilds, and void-corruption — everything D&D players recognise, run on Fate Condensed. The world is legally complicated and physically dangerous in equal measure.

Tone & Atmosphere

High fantasy for D&D converts. The Senate's collapse created a legal and institutional vacuum that guilds, temples, dragons, and liches are all filling in incompatible ways. Everything D&D players recognise — dungeon delving, guild contracts, arcane magic, monster treaties — runs on Fate Condensed's aspect/stunt/stress framework. The dungeons are real. The contracts are binding. The d20 has twenty sides for a reason.

For Players

You are dungeon delvers, guild contractors, void-touched mercenaries, and kobold crew bosses navigating a world where every job has a contract and every contract has a penalty clause. Your class is your High Concept. Your spell is a stunt. Your dungeon licence covers you for the first three floors.

Coming from D&D?

This world was built for you. Sneak Attack, Divine Smite, Bardic Inspiration, Wild Shape, Counterspell — all 28 stunts are named after D&D class features and translated to Fate mechanics. The vocabulary is the same. The system underneath is leaner. No action economy. No hit points. Just aspects, stress, and consequences.

Key Factions

The Guild Network (contracts, licences, arbitration), the Temple Compact (divine authority filling the Senate's administrative void), Dragon Flight Territories (treaty rights, tribute, and very long memories), the Lich Underground (post-Senate magicians operating outside the licensing system), Goblin Mercenary Unions (organised, opinionated, contractually protected).

Inspirations

D&D 5e (any setting), Pathfinder, Dragon Age, The Dresden Files, Discworld, Critical Role
