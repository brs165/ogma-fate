Blue-Collar Space Western

The Colonial Transit Authority controls the jump routes, taxes the fuel, and enforces the law when profitable. Everyone else is trying to make the ship payment. The fuel monopoly means every job is a calculation. The Ghost Fleet suggests someone is building something. The Signal - coordinates and a date - suggests someone is expecting something.

Tone & Atmosphere

Working-class science fiction. Ships held together by spit and the mechanic's refusal to admit what the warranty doesn't cover. Space is expensive, dangerous, and governed by people who have never been there.

For Players

You are crew. Your ship is your home, your livelihood, and your prison. The CTA wants transit fees. Pirates want cargo. Corporations want labour. Jump transit takes forty minutes of sensory deprivation and nobody comes out quite the same.

Key Factions

Colonial Transit Authority (CTA - Navigation Bureau vs. Enforcement Division), pirate fleets, corporate interests (fuel monopoly, salvage), free ports (independent stations), ghost fleet operators (unknown, well-funded).

Inspirations

Firefly, The Expanse, Cowboy Bebop, Alien, Outlaw Star, Killjoys
