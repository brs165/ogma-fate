◈ The Long After

Sword-and-Planet Dying Earth

The world ended. The world that replaced it thinks the debris is magic. Ancient Phade vaults litter a landscape of sand, fungal forests, and city-states ruled by warlords who claim divine authority because they can read the error messages. Delvers descend into the ruins to recover what was lost. The vaults are not empty. The technology is not dead.

Tone & Atmosphere

Pulp adventure with literary depth. The action is broad - sky-chases, arena fights, beast-riding - but the world asks real questions about technology, authority, and building civilisation on someone else's ruins. The Phade are not gods. The warlords know this. The people do not.

For Players

You are delvers, wanderers, and rebels in a world where understanding is the most valuable commodity. Phade technology grants power but changes those who use it. Every vault teaches you something - and something in the vault learns about you.

Key Factions

Warlord states (military theocracies built on partial Phade knowledge), delver crews (scavenger-scholars), arena circuits (blood-sport economies), Phade cults (true believers in the old gods' return), sky-city remnants (last survivors of the old civilisation).

Inspirations

Nausicaä of the Valley of the Wind, The Book of the New Sun, Thundarr the Barbarian, Horizon Zero Dawn, She-Ra, The Dying Earth
