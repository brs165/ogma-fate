At the Table

Running your session with Ogma

Ogma is designed for session prep — generating content, keeping results, and exporting them to your table. This guide covers the tools that make prep fast and seamless.

Fate Point Tracker

Click the ◎ button in the action bar to open the FP Tracker. It has three tabs:

  • Fate Points — tracks the party pool (shared points), per-PC points, and per-NPC points. Each PC has a Refresh value; clicking ↺ resets everyone to refresh.
  • Milestones — Milestone (end of every session) and Breakthrough (end of a story arc) checklists. Check items off as the group achieves them; tick marks animate.
  • Popcorn Initiative — the Balsera-style turn order. Add participants, then pass the token by clicking "Goes next →". No fixed turn order — the acting character picks who goes next.
🎪 Scenario: Tracking a tense contest

You've rolled a Contest — two gangs racing to reach the weapons cache. As each exchange resolves, you tap the victory boxes on your screen. The left track (Crimson Scars) and right track (Iron Saints) fill up. On the third tie, the twist aspect — "The Cache is Already Open" — gets revealed. Export the final state as Ogma JSON so players can see it on their devices.

Inspiration Mode

Press I to enter Inspiration Mode. Ogma rolls three results simultaneously. They display as overlapping ghost cards. Click the one you want to use — the others fade out. If none work, press I again for three more. Useful when a single roll isn't sparking anything creative.

Quick Prep Pack prep packet

In the Prep tab of the sidebar, click Quick Prep Pack. Ogma generates three connected cards simultaneously:

  • Adventure Seed — location, objective, complication, 3-scene sketch
  • Countdown — a threat clock already ticking from session start
  • Compel — a ready-to-offer complication for the first scene

These three cards together are a Session 1 prep packet — enough to run 2–3 hours of play with no other prep. Click "Keep all" to save all three to your session at once.

History and your session

Every roll goes into a history ring (last 8 results). Kept cards persist until you remove them. Open History with the 📋 button in the action bar or press the keyboard shortcut.

From History you can:

  • Re-view any card in full
  • Remove individual cards
  • Copy all saved cards as a single Markdown document
  • Export all saved results as a single Ogma JSON file (re-importable in any Ogma campaign)

Press Z to immediately undo the last keep without opening History.

Table Prep — save, load, and share

Table Prep is your persistent storage. Open it from the Prep tab → 📋 Saved Prep. It lets you:

  • Save — give your current set of saved cards a name and save them. They persist across browser sessions.
  • Load — click any saved session to add its cards to your current session.
  • Export — download any session as a .json file. This is the share mechanism — drop it in Discord, email it, put it on a USB stick.
  • Import — load a .json file from another GM or another device into your session.
  • Per-card export — in the History panel, each pinned card has a ↓ button that exports just that card as JSON.
Tip

Saved Prep stores everything in your browser's IndexedDB. It works fully offline. JSON files are the way to move sessions between devices or share with other GMs.

Campaign Guide pages

Each campaign world has a dedicated Guide page (accessible from the sidebar under Navigate → Campaign Guide). These pages contain:

  • World tone and atmosphere
  • Player-facing setting summary
  • Key factions
  • Full content overview (table entry counts by category)

Guide pages are printable — use Ctrl+P to print a reference sheet for your players.
