How to use Ogma

Three paths depending on what you need right now. Each takes about 2 minutes to read.

📜
Path 1: Use Ogma to prep a session
You're a GM getting ready for game night. You want material you can use at the table.
  1. Pick a campaign world — choose one of the 8 worlds from the landing page, or use any world's generators for setting-agnostic content.
  2. Pick a generator — the sidebar groups all 16 generators by category: NPCs, Scenes, Campaign, Encounters, and Toolbox. Start with whatever you need most.
  3. Roll and read — tap the Roll button (or press Space). Read the result. If you like it, tap the ★ keep button. If not, roll again.
  4. Build up your session — pin 4-6 cards: a scene, an NPC or two, an encounter, maybe a compel hook. Each roll gives you a self-contained piece of prep.
  5. Export — open the History panel to see all saved cards. Copy as Markdown, save as Ogma JSON, download a session .md file, or share a seed link.
🎧
Path 2: Use Ogma as a GM at the table
Your session is live. You need something right now — an NPC name, a scene twist, an encounter complication.
  1. Open your campaign page — if you prepped earlier, your saved cards are still there (saved automatically).
  2. Export to your table — use Ogma JSON to save and re-import results, print/PDF for physical cards, or Markdown to share with other GMs.
  3. Track fate points — open the Session tab in the sidebar for the FP tracker (GM pool = 1 per PC), Popcorn Initiative tracker, and Milestone/Breakthrough checklists.
  4. Generate on the fly — need a complication? A countdown? A quick NPC? Roll mid-session. The generators are designed for at-the-table speed.
  5. Check "How to use this" — every result has an expandable reference section below it with the mechanic, running tips, and invoke/compel examples.
🎲
Path 3: Use Ogma as a player
Your GM uses Ogma. Here's what you need to know.
  1. Ask your GM for exports — your GM can share session content via Ogma JSON, printed cards, or Markdown files. Ogma generates the content; your table tool of choice displays it.
  2. Explore the help pages — the Fate Mechanics page explains stress, consequences, aspects, and fate points. The D&D Transition Guide helps if you're coming from D&D.
  3. Try Session Zero — the Session Zero Wizard walks through collaborative character creation step by step. Your GM may use it to guide the group.
Tip

The info tabs below every generated result (What · How · In play) answer the three questions you'll have about any generator. Start with "What" to understand the mechanic, read "How" for GM running tips, and check "In play" for concrete invoke/compel examples.
