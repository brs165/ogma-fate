Generator Suite

16 generators. 8 worlds. 128 combinations.

Every generator produces a mechanically complete, thematically correct Fate Condensed result. Each one is tuned to its campaign world — a Minor NPC in Shattered Kingdoms gets dark fantasy names and aspects; the same generator in Neon Abyss gets chrome and neon.

How generators and campaigns combine

When you roll, Ogma pulls from two table layers:

  • Campaign-specific tables — names, aspects, factions, and world details tuned to your chosen world (e.g. Shattered Kingdoms' "Blight-touched" aspects).
  • Universal tables — shared mechanical content that works in any genre (stress thresholds, consequence severity, skill ladders). The Universal Merge toggle in Settings controls whether these supplement or replace campaign tables.

Characters

👤
Minor NPC
Name, 2 aspects (high concept + weakness), 1 skill, stress track. Ready in one roll — no prep needed.
👑
Major NPC
Full character: HC, Trouble, 3 aspects, 5 skills, 2 stunts, physical/mental stress, consequences, refresh.
📝
PC Backstory
Session Zero questions that generate aspects organically. Also includes a relationship web prompt and an opening hook.
🎪 Scenario: Building a recurring villain

Select Major NPC and roll. You get a complete character: maybe "The Archivist of Broken Oaths" (high concept), "Debts I Cannot Repay" (trouble), three supporting aspects, ranked skills with ladder labels, two stunts (one +2 bonus, one once-per-scene), and a refresh of 3. (Two stunts sit within the three free stunt slots — no refresh penalty.) The For GM tab tells you to show their high concept in action before the PCs engage — let them win something small first so the stakes feel real.

Scenes

🌟
Scene Setup
2–4 situation aspects in categories (tone, cover, movement, danger, usable), plus 3–5 named zones. Tap aspects to mark them active during play.
⚔
Encounter
Victory condition, defeat condition, situation aspect, 3–5 opposition NPCs with stress. Designed to run the whole encounter from one card.
⚡
Complication
A new aspect + optional new arrival + environmental shift. For when the scene needs a surprise mid-conflict.

Pacing

⚡
Challenge
Named challenge, description, success condition, failure condition, optional scene aspect. The full Fate Challenge structure in one card.
⚔
Contest
Two sides, victory track (first to 3), situation aspect, 3 twist aspects for tied exchanges. Live score tracking — tap boxes to fill them.
🚫
Obstacle
Named obstacle, aspect, type (hazard/block/distraction), disable/bypass condition, opposition rating.
⏰
Countdown
Named clock, 4–8 boxes, trigger condition, outcome. Tap boxes to fill them — particles fire when the clock hits zero.
🚫
Constraint
Limitation or resistance. Limitations restrict an action; resistances require a bypass. Each includes a how-to-bypass.

World

🌎
Campaign Frame
Current issue + impending issue + 3 setting aspects. Your campaign's backbone in one roll.
🌐
Adventure Seed
Location, objective, complication, victory condition, defeat condition, 3-scene sketch. Run Session 1 from this card.
🏆
Faction
Name, goal, method, weakness, named face NPC. Gives you a faction you can immediately compel.
💡
Compel
Situation + consequence. Hands the GM a ready-to-offer compel for any scene.
🩹
Consequence
Severity (Mild/Moderate/Severe), named aspect, context. Includes recovery guidance and treatment difficulty.

The result panel

Every result card has a two-column reference sheet directly below it — always visible, no click required:

  • What it is (left column) — what this generator produces and the relevant Fate Condensed rule with a direct SRD link.
  • For GM (right column) — imperative running tips, invoke and compel examples from the FCon SRD and Book of Hanz.

Two tabs appear below every result. What this is · For GM shows what the generator produces and running tips. ⚔ D&D? (when available) gives a side-by-side comparison for players coming from D&D or Pathfinder.

  • What · For GM — the default 2-column view above.
  • 🌱 New here? — plain-language explanation for players new to Fate, with key term definitions.
  • ⚔ D&D? — side-by-side comparison of Fate and D&D mechanics for GMs switching systems.

Pinning results

Press P or click the Save to Table Prep button on any result to pin it. Open pinned cards from the Binder section in the sidebar.

From History you can: re-view any card, copy or download as Markdown, export your whole session as an Ogma JSON file, or import a previously saved .ogma.json file.

Shareable links

Press L or click the 🔗 button to copy a shareable URL for the current result. The URL encodes a seed that reproduces the exact same result — share it in Discord, paste it into session notes, or send it to your co-GM. Anyone who opens the link sees the same card.

Inspiration mode

Press I to roll three results at once and pick the one you want. The other two fade as ghost cards. Great when a single roll isn't sparking anything — seeing three options simultaneously often unlocks the right direction.

Quick Prep Pack prep

In the Binder section of the sidebar, click Quick Prep Pack to generate an Adventure Seed + Countdown + Compel as a stacked 3-card packet. This is your Session 1 prep in one click — location, first threat, and a ready-to-offer complication.

Table Manager

Click the ⊞ icon in the action bar to open the Table Manager. You can:

  • Exclude any table from generation (e.g. if you've already used that faction name)
  • Lock a table to always use that entry (e.g. fix a PC's name)
  • Add custom entries that sit alongside the built-in content
