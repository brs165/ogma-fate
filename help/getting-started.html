Getting Started

Your first session with Ogma

Ogma is ready to use the moment you open it — no login, no setup, no internet after first load. This guide explains how it works and what you need to know to use it safely.

The local-first philosophy

Ogma stores everything in your browser — there is no server, no cloud sync, no account. This is a deliberate design choice: it means the tool works offline, loads instantly, and your data is private by default.

Specifically, Ogma uses two browser storage mechanisms:

  • IndexedDB — stores your session state: last result, saved cards, FP tracker state, history ring. This persists across browser restarts.
  • localStorage — stores your preferences: theme, universal merge. Table customisations and session data use IndexedDB for reliability.
⚠ Before you clear your browser cache

Clearing your browser cache, cookies, or site data will permanently delete your session history, pinned results, FP tracker state, and table customisations. There is no server backup. Always export your pinned results as Markdown before clearing storage.

The service worker — offline first

On your first visit, Ogma installs a service worker that caches all 740KB of assets (HTML, CSS, JS, fonts, data) to your device. After that, the tool works with no internet connection at all — you can run it in a cabin with no signal.

When a new version of Ogma is deployed, the service worker detects the change and shows an "Update available — reload?" toast. Clicking it reloads with the latest version. Your data is not affected by updates.

📷 Install as a PWA

Ogma is a Progressive Web App. On desktop Chrome/Edge, look for an install icon in the address bar. On iOS Safari, tap Share → Add to Home Screen. This gives you a dedicated app icon and a cleaner full-screen experience.

Your first roll

  1. Pick a generatorUse the sidebar on the left — generators are grouped into Characters, Scenes, Pacing, and World. Start with Minor NPC for something immediately useful.
  2. RollPress Space or click the big Roll button. A complete, mechanically-correct result appears in the right panel.
  3. Read the resultThe result card shows the full output. Below it, the GM Tips tab gives you specific table advice for this generator. The Rules tab shows the relevant Fate Condensed mechanic.
  4. Keep it if you want to save itPress P or click the Save to Table Prep button. The result goes to Table Prep (📋 in the action bar) where you can review or export it later.
🎪 Scenario: You need a quick NPC mid-session

Your players just knocked on an unexpected door. Press Space with Minor NPC selected. A name, two aspects (high concept + weakness), skill level, and stress track appear in under a second. The result is thematically appropriate to your campaign world — no generic fantasy names in a cyberpunk game. Press P to pin it, V to show it on your second screen.

Backing up your work

Ogma does not have an auto-sync or cloud backup. To preserve your work:

  • Keep results you want to save — press P after any roll.
  • Export pinned results — open History (📋), then use the "Copy all as Markdown" button to get a full Markdown document of every pinned card.
  • Download the ZIP — from the About page, download the full Ogma ZIP file. This is the entire app as a folder you can run from your desktop with no internet ever.

Offline use

Ogma works fully offline after your first visit. The service worker caches all assets automatically — generators, campaign data, styles, and scripts. No internet required after the initial load. You can also download the zip and serve it locally with npx serve or any static web server.
